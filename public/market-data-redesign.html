BOT MONITORING Design exploration · Order book + live chart panels

Three ways to redesign the "what the bot is watching" zone

Today the chart shows price only, yet the footer says "Watching for Bollinger upper band cross + RSI < 70" — the bot's trigger conditions are invisible. The order book sits beside it, but its connection to the trigger isn't shown either. Three directions explore: overlay the rules, merge into one HUD, or put the trigger state first. All use the existing token palette and real numbers from the screenshot.

Assumptions used (click to expand)
  • Pair = ETH-USDC. Mid ≈ $2,299.45. Spread $3.90 / 0.170%.
  • Trigger rule = Bollinger upper band cross + RSI < 70 (from current footer).
  • BB period 20 std-dev 2 (default in registry); RSI period 14.
  • Panels are full-width in the monitoring page (~1280px content).
  • Order book Hyperliquid L2, top 6 levels each side, refresh ~1s.
V1

Lightweight: overlay the watched indicators directly on the chart

Smallest delta. Keep both cards but make the rule visible: Bollinger envelope on the chart, slim RSI strip beneath, "Trigger watcher" chip in the corner that turns yellow when conditions converge. Order book gets a horizontal spread strip (no more middle column).

V2

Merged HUD: depth becomes a vertical gauge attached to the chart

Two cards collapse into one tall "market HUD." Chart is hero, with Bollinger envelope and trigger thresholds drawn as horizontal annotated lines. The order book transforms into a vertical depth ladder pinned to the right of the chart — each price level becomes a bar; you read liquidity walls visually instead of as a number column. Spread + mid float as a small HUD chip in the top-right.

V3

Action-first: trigger watcher is hero, market data is secondary

Flip the hierarchy. The user's mental model on this page is "is my bot about to fire?" — answer that first. Big "Watching" hero card breaks the rule into per-condition progress bars + "how close" thermometer. Chart + order book compress to a row below as supporting evidence. Cypheus-style narrative line under the hero ties it together.

Recommendation

  • Ship V1 first — same two cards, just add Bollinger overlay + RSI strip + status footer. Closes the biggest perception gap with the least rebuild.
  • Consider V3 as a follow-up once Cypheus narrative is wired in; the hero card naturally hosts the "what's the bot thinking" voice.
  • V2 is the prettiest but the heaviest — defer unless you want this page to feel "pro terminal."
